About the legend

Life:
Birth: Born on December 25, 1876, in Karachi, British India.
Education: Studied law in London and became a barrister in 1896.

Political Career:
Initial Involvement: Joined the Indian National Congress in 1906 but later shifted focus to advocate for Muslim rights.
All-India Muslim League: Became a key member in 1913, establishing a political platform for Muslims.

Two-Nation Theory:
Jinnah articulated the concept of the Two-Nation Theory, emphasizing the distinct identity and rights of Muslims and Hindus.

Pakistan Movement:
Lahore Resolution: In 1940, he led the Lahore Resolution, which called for an independent nation for Muslims.
Independence: On August 14, 1947, Pakistan was established, with Jinnah as its first Governor-General.

Leadership Style:
His leadership was marked by hard work, determination, and a clear vision, uniting Muslims across the subcontinent.
The motto "Unity, Faith, and Discipline" became synonymous with his leadership.
